What Is a CV Document?
CV stands for Curriculum Vitae or "course of life: In the US, a CV is a 2-12 page academic document that describes your school career in deep detail. In Europe, a CV is a standard 1-2 page resume for a job, tailored to the position. In other words, the meaning and definition of a CV depends on your location. Here's a quick rundown of both kinds of CVs and their definitions:
What is an American CV?
In the United States, a CV document is an exhaustively comprehensive rundown of your entire academic career. It's massive, like a federal resume federal resume or academic cv. Since it's not tailored to a specific job opening, it adds everything universities might care about. That means every publication, conference, and school projec you've done.
What Does CV Mean in England?
In Europe and Ireland, a CV is a resume for a job. It's 1-2 pages long. It doesn't list every achievement. Instead, it touts accomplishments that prove key skills needed for the job. What is a CV for Jobs?
A CV for jobs is the some as a resume for jobs. Europeans and New Zealanders call it a CV and Americans call it a resume. Either way, CV meaning is the same. It's just a 1-2 page document showing skills and experience.
How to Write a CV for Jobs
Hold up a sec.
If you're in America, don't write a job CV. Write a resume instead. Need help? See our guide: How to Make a Resume for a Job (from Application to Interview in 24h). If you're in the US and writing an academic CV, see the section below. If you're applying for a job in Europe or New Zealand, here's how to write a job CV:
  • Choose the right CV format
  • Lead off with your contact info.
  • Write a CV summary.
  • Add tailored work experience and achievements.
  • Include an education section that fits the job.
  • Salt in bonus CV sections to wow recruiters.
  • Save your work in PDF form.
